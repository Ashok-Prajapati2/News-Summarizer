Synopsis

Under this contract, Pixxel will provide NASA, the US government and its academic partners with hyperspectral Earth observation data, further strengthening the administration’s Earth science research and application activities.

Bengaluru-based hyperspectral imaging and earth observation company has secured a contract from as part of its $476 million Commercial SmallSat Data Acquisition Programme, Nasa and Pixxel said in simultaneous announcements on Monday.

Under this contract, Pixxel will provide Nasa, the US government and its academic partners with hyperspectral Earth observation data, strengthening the administration’s and application activities.

With the ability to capture data across hundreds of narrow wavelengths, Pixxel’s datasets can unravel granular insights on climate change, agriculture, biodiversity, resource management and more, the company said.

"Being selected for this Nasa contract is a monumental achievement for Pixxel and further validates that hyperspectral imaging will be integral to the future of space-based Earth observation and enable us to truly build a health monitor for the planet," said Awais Ahmed, co-founder and chief executive of Pixxel.

The contract’s period of performance runs through November 2028.

Pixxel also said it was the youngest company in a group that includes some of the most established names globally in earth observation. The other companies include BlackSky Geospatial Solutions, ICEYE US, MDA Geospatial Service, Planet Labs Federal, Satellogic Federal, Teledyne Brown Engineering and The Tomorrow Companies.

Pixxel's upcoming launch includes Fireflies, its five-meter resolution hyperspectral satellites, which will be the highest-resolution hyperspectral satellites ever launched, according to the company. These satellites will capture data across more than 250 spectral bands, offering more comprehensive coverage with a 40 km swath width and a 24-hour revisit frequency anywhere on the planet.

Along with the six upcoming satellites launching shortly, Pixxel plans to expand its constellation to 24 satellites to make hyperspectral data commercially available and accessible to stakeholders across industries and governments. This will empower them with real-time, actionable insights to informed decision-making, improve natural resource management and enhance environmental resilience, the company said.
