Maharashtra commences second phase of teacher recruitment

In February 2023, the state's school education department started recruiting teachers for 21,678 vacant positions through Pavitra portal—a tech-based centralised platform for the process.

By: Mumbai | Updated: September 15, 2024 15:17 IST
2 min read
Teachers were asked to report to respective schools for the final stage of the process, including offer-letter and joining. (File Photo)

The Maharashtra school education department has commenced the second phase of teachers’ recruitment in government and aided schools. A review will first be taken of available vacant seats, apart from 10 per cent posts reserved from the first phase of the process. After the review, a final number of available posts for rectruit ment will be declared.

In February 2023, the state’s school education for 21,678 vacant positions through Pavitra portal—a tech-based centralised platform for the process, where advertisements are declared on details of vacant posts and qualified candidates apply.

Through the process, as many as 19,986 eligible candidates were recommended as qualified. Accordingly, teachers were asked to report to respective schools for the final stage of the process, including offer-letter and joining.

Advertisement

Commissioner of Education, Suraj Mandhare, said, “All candidates do not take up the recommended jobs, so a few are left vacant. Additionally, there will be more vacant posts with existing teachers retiring. The respective district level education authorities are now asked to take a review of the situation of vacant posts and then we will declare the number of posts available for the second phase of recruitment.”

Mandhare has issued a letter regarding this to all regional education officers. It states, “Recruitment on 10 per cent reserved posts from the first phase has to be conducted. But there may be additional vacant seats as candidates did not accept the allotted job in the first phase. Information of all such posts as per subject-wise requirement should be prepared.”

From the total 21,678 posts available for employment in the first phase, 10 per cent were kept reserved due to pending clarity in reservation posts at some levels. These will be added in the second phase, along with new posts.
